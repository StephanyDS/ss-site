The Google Search App

Beginning in 2014, I became the lead Producer for our agency's largest client, the Google Search App. With the goal of increasing daily installs of new users, as well as increasing DAU of existing users, we created a multitude of Awareness, Engagement and mobile DR campaigns to meet those objectives over the course of two years. The ongoing results were - and continue to be - successful in promoting download and usage across the board.

  • Client: Google Search
  • Date: September 2014
  • Service: Digital Strategy, Creative Production, Project Management

Google Maps and Smarty Pins

Originally pitched as fun trivia game using the Maps API to increase awareness and usage of the Google Maps app, Smarty Pins quickly took on a life of its own. With nearly 3,000 pieces of trivia content over multiple continents, development aside, the task of content management quickly became a monster challenge, headed up by yours truly! With minimal paid media behind the launch, the last minute integration of shareable features also presented an uphill battle against timelines and development deadlines, but we got it done. With over 3MM plays, and over a dozen press relseases in the firt week, the entire effort was a resounding success not only for the Maps team, but throughout Google as well.

  • Client: Google Maps
  • Date: May 2014
  • Service: Content Strategy and Management, International Project Management, Technical and Creative Production

Toyota 100 Cars for Good

The project I am most proud of in my career to date, 100 Cars for Good was the first and largest philanthropic endeavor ever undertaken by Toyota USA. The premise was that nonprofits would submit their charities for consideration for a free vehicle of their choosing, and then over the course of 100 days, Facebook users (back when Facebook supported canvas apps!) would vote for one out of five each day to win. An unexpected success in the wake of the previous year's recall, the program ran for three years, totaling 300 cars given away to worthy nonprofits. I personally was one of two leads on this, directly responsible for the site and CMS build, content creation, nonprofit relations, and overall campaign success.

  • Client: Toyota Motor Sales, USA
  • Date: December 2010
  • Service: Scope, Strategy, Client/Dealer/Nonprofit Relations, Digital/Video Production

Toyota Owners Site

It's always fun when a client says, "Let's blow this thing up and start from scratch." and that's just what Toyota did. The old Toyota Owner's site was wonky, out of date, and had little business value to either the acquisition or retention teams. I led senior and C-level stakeholder interviews to gather business requirements, and then consolidated and prioritized those for the larger development team. Then we got to work on the first true Agile-led development build in the agency's history (Scrum certifications and all!). The site now offers near real-time information on vehicle diagnostics, upcoming service milestones, parts and service inventory, and any news related to a user's vehicle(s), including what those damn warning lights really mean.

  • Client: Toyota Motor Sales, USA
  • Date: June 2010
  • Service: Scope, Content Strategy, Content Management, Account Management, Project Management, Maintenance Planning
